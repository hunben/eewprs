Gandhápakarśaka.(1) Gandhápakarśaka means [a particular type of sweet-scented wood]. In ancient times, people would use this type of wood as a fuel in sacrificial fires to produce an aromatic smoke which would remove all odours, germs and diseases, and negative microvita.

Footnotes

(1) Other meanings of gandhápakarśaka omitted here. –Trans.

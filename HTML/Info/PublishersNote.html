Publisher's Note

The Works of P.R. Sarkar

Between 1955 and 1990 Shrii Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar, whose spiritual name was Shrii Shrii Ánandamúrti, through his discourses and dictations produced material for what has now become a total of 268 books, some of them orginally published in Bengali, some in English, and some in Hindi. He wrote in the name Shrii Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar when treating sociology, economics, philology, history and various other subjects, and when writing children's tales; and in the name Shrii Shrii Ánandamúrti when focusing on spiritual topics. Shrii Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar was the name in which his 5018 Prabháta Saḿgiita songs were published. Those songs, mostly in Bengali, were first published in a series of small booklets, and have now been compiled into ten large Bengali volumes. Translation of those ten volumes into a 25-volume English series is under way, hence Prabháta Saḿgiita (though for the most part not yet available in English) is counted as 25 books in the reckoning of 268.

138 of the 268 books are now available in English. All of them except the five most recent and the two available books that contain Prabháta Saḿgiita songs have been compiled into this Electronic Edition. (Prabháta Saḿgiita, text and audio, will in future be presented separately on another CD.) For more specific remarks about the contents of the various books, see their respective Publisher's Notes within this edition.

The figures of 268 and 138 are for books originally published by Ananda Marga Central Publications. Seven English compilations not originally published by Ananda Marga Central Publications, rather by Ananda Marga Publications at other levels but now the property of Ananda Marga Central Publications and also included in the ElEdit, are Bábá's Grace, The Great Universe, Supreme Expression Volume 1, Supreme Expression Volume 2, Bábá in Fiesch, Bábá in Taiwan and Universal Humanism. Virtually all of their contents duplicates material that has been included among the 138.

ElEdit 7.5

The ElEdit 7 contained all the 1207 discourses and articles by Shrii P.R. Sarkar that had, as of that time, ever been published in English. ("Discourse" here means that the author originally delivered it as a speech before an audience. "Article" means that the author gave it as dictation to one or a few persons. "Published in English" means that the discourse/article was either originally given in English, or subsequently translated from the original Bengali or Hindi. The Publisher's Note of the ElEdit 7 gave a figure of 1224 discourses and articles; but 17 Prabháta Saḿgiita songs had mistakenly been counted as discourses, so the figure should have been 1207.) This Version 7.5 includes five books newly-published since Version 7, bringing the count of discourses/articles to 1266; and one book that was being reprinted included, as a new article, a portion of an article already existing in another book, bringing the count to 1267. The 7.5 also includes five new discourses or articles never before published in any language, bringing the count to 1272; and meanwhile, one article was dropped from a book that was being reprinted, since the material was duplicated elsewhere, and hence has been dropped from the ElEdit also. So the final count of discourses/articles in this ElEdit 7.5 is 1271.

The five new discourses or articles never before published in any language: "The Vital Energy of Students", "Subtler than the Subtlest Mind", "Movement Is the Order of the Day", "The Philosophical Interpretation of Progress" and "August 2nd, 1988".

The count of 1271 includes as one discourse or article each the long discourses Problems of the Day and To the Patriots; Discourses on Prout, which was really a series of discourses; A Guide to Human Conduct, which is divided into sections; and Sarkar's English Grammar. Many book chapters in the count of discourses/articles/chapters are really excerpts or compilations of excerpts from other discourses; to pare the figure of 1271 to an exact count of original discourses/articles would require a very detailed calculation. Light Comes, a collection of short excerpts published as a book, is counted as one discourse; and The Thoughts of P.R. Sarkar, a similar book, is counted as two discourses ("Spiritual Discourses" and "Social Discourses").

DMC and RU

Normally the most important of the author's discourses on spiritual topics were given on the occasions of dharma mahácakra. Dharma mahácakra was a special spiritual gathering addressed by the author as preceptor of Ananda Marga. And often the most important of the author's discourses on topics related more to the physical universe than to the spiritual, were his addresses as the president of the organization Renaissance Universal. Dharma mahácakra discourses and Renaissance Universal addresses can be identified by the codes "DMC" and "RU" among the notes at the bottom of each article or discourse.

Corrections

Only the latest printed versions (as of October 2009) of all the discourses/articles have been reproduced in this Electronic Edition. Moreover, as compared to the printed books, corrections of mistakes in translation or in tape transcription have been made in many discourses/articles. All such changes have been flagged by enclosing them in double square brackets [[   ]]. In addition to such significant changes that have been flagged, obvious spelling, punctuation and typographical mistakes may have been corrected without any such flagging. If apart from corrections of obvious spelling, punctuation and typographical mistakes, any discrepancies between the ElEdit and the printed books are noticed which have not been flagged, the printed books should be taken as authoritative. Please report any such discrepancies to us at support@eledit.net, preferably with a copy to translat@cal3.vsnl.net.in

Source Information

For information about the publication history of any discourse, click (at the top or bottom of the discourse) on the title of the book in which that discourse was published; this will take you to the table of contents of that book. Then see if there is a Publisher's Note for that book. If there is, it may contain source information.

Notes

In the Notes at the top of a discourse or article, you will often see that the discourse/article has appeared in a number of books, and that some of the books are compilations of articles from other books. In such cases, the book which is not described as a compilation is considered to be the official source of that discourse/article.

Etymologies of Words

The author used a certain shorthand for explaining the etymologies of words. Under this system, a minus sign (-) follows a prefix, and a plus sign (+) precedes a suffix. Thus ava - tr + ghaiṋ = avatára can be read, "the root tr prefixed by ava and suffixed by ghaiṋ becomes avatára."

Acknowledgements

The English translation and editing of the works of Shrii P.R. Sarkar accomplished to date has been made possible by a great number of persons, almost all of whom volunteered their time and energy, over a period of fifty-four years. It would not be practical to name all such persons here; nor would it even be possible, because full records have not been kept of this history of dedication. Despite the exhausting labour sometimes involved in producing publications materials of good quality, all such persons felt that it was a rare privilege to be allowed to do this work. Perhaps they felt that it was they who owed an acknowledgement to the author. But society should acknowledge its debt to these persons, even if they remain nameless, and heedless of recognition.

Some English Translation Statistics (updated 1 October 09)

As of April '91, we calculated the writings of Ba'ba' (as Shrii P.R. Sarkar is affectionately known), published or scheduled for publication (scheduled, that is, on the basis of known discourses as yet unpublished in book form), to come to 419 titles.* (Between some titles there was considerable repetition of material.)

* The 419 included P.R. Sarkar on History, A'darsh Bhojpuri Vya'ka'ran' [Ideal Bhojpuri Grammar] and a book on Angika grammar. P.R. Sarkar on History was originally included among the Ba'ba' books scheduled for publication, and is now, though it eventually came out in Da'da' Raghuna'th's name, included in the 419 as one of Ba'ba''s published books. A'darsh Bhojpuri Vya'ka'ran', though written by Da'da' Shraddha'nanda, was written basically according to instructions given by Ba'ba', hence is also included. A book on Angika grammar similarly commissioned by Ba'ba' has not been written yet, but was inadvertently included when these statistics were first compiled, hence continues to be included for statistical convenience.

That 419 base began to expand in October '94, based partly on rediscovered darshans -- some from tapes, some from old magazines and notes -- and partly on new compilation of existing material. Based on rediscovered darshans, we now have A'nanda Vacana'mrtam Part 24 (Bengali), Parts 25-29 and 32 (Hindi), and Parts 30, 31, 33 and 34 (English); Subha's'ita Sam'graha Parts 22, 23 and 25 (Hindi) and Part 24 (English); and Jiivana Diipika' (Bengali). Based on new compilation, we have (in Bengali) the books Bhaktirasa o Kiirtana-Mahima' [The Flow of Bhakti and the Bliss of Kiirtana], Ba'm'la'r Man'ipradiip Barendrabhu'mi [Barendrabhu'mi: The Jewel-Lamp of Bengal], Prakrt Guru Ke? [The Criteria of a Guru], Dravyagun'e Roga'rogya [Natural Medicine] and Pra'ut'er Ru'parekha' [An Outline of Prout]; (in Bengali and English) the books Krs'n'atattva o Giita'sa'ra (Discourses on Krs'n'a and the Giita'), Na'riir Marya'da' (The Awakening of Women) and Discourses on Neohumanist Education (Navyama'navata'va'd Shiks'a'); and (in English) the book Prabha'ta Sam'giita Selected Songs Part 1. 419 + 25 = in all 444 titles. 7 of the original 419 were considered untranslatable (there would be no purpose in translating them) -- e.g., Ba'ba''s Bengali dictionary. So regarding English, the base of books already available in English, or scheduled ultimately for English publication, was 412 and has now grown to 437. Calculating the Pace of the English Work

36 new English titles (translations or original English) have come out since April '91: Proutist Economics, Tantra 1 and 2, Discourses on Prout, SS 11, 12, 18, 19, 21 and 24, A'nanda Vacana'mrtam 9-11, 23, 30, 31, 33 and 34, P.R. Sarkar on History, The Awakening of Women, Shabda Cayanika' 1-5, Neohumanist Education, Discourses on Krs'n'a and the Giita', Varn'a Vijina'na, Strange Experiences, Ra'r'h: The Cradle of Civilization, Prabha'ta Sam'giita Selected Songs Part 1, Sam'giita: Song, Dance and Instrumental Music, Birds and Animals Our Neighbours, and Histories along the Way 1-3. (Of course as far as work during that 18-1/2-year period, there have also been many reprints/retranslations -- Problems of the Day, To the Patriots, Yoga Psychology (three times revised), Idea & Ideology, English Grammar, Namah Shiva'ya Sha'nta'ya, Carya'carya 1, A'nanda Su'tram, Nama'mi Krs'n'asundaram, A'nanda Ma'rga: Elementary Philosophy, Human Society 1 and 2, Liberation of Intellect, Neohumanism in a Nutshell 1, Yogic Treatments (twice revised), A'nanda Vacana'mrtam 12 -- but these have not affected the statistics.) For purposes of a future projection, it would be best to remember that out of the overall total of 444 titles, 201, as mentioned above, are small Bengali Prabha'ta Sam'giita booklets; and therefore to adjust the figures in terms of the full-size books that will appear in English. The PS booklets will be published in English eight to a volume (a full-size book); they will constitute 25 full-size books. In these terms the overall total comes to 444 - 201 + 25 = 268 books (7 of them untranslatable), and in these terms the above table (which focuses on the English) would appear as follows:

* P.R. Sarkar on History was originally included among the 18 Ba'ba' books due for original publication in English, and is now, though it came out in Da'da' Raghuna'th's name, included among the figure of 138 in English.

141 + 18 = 159 full-size books still to come out in English as of '91. 36 have actually come out in those 18-1/2 years, leaving 123 still to go. 123 is a strict figure according to number of untranslated titles. But many of the individual articles in those books have come out in different English compilations; and as mentioned, among some titles there is considerable repetition of material (it would be very detailed to calculate just how much); so a realistic figure would be much smaller. And we expect the time spent on reprints/retranslations to decrease somewhat.

Under an accelerated-translation project initiated in July of 2005, the pace of work has been one book every five months.

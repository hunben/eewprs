Publisher's Note

The Works of P.R. Sarkar

Between 1955 and 1990 Shrii Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar, whose spiritual name was Shrii Shrii Ánandamúrti, through his discourses and dictations produced material for what has now become a total of 269 books in Bengali, English and Hindi. He wrote in the name Shrii Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar when treating sociology, economics, philology and various other subjects, and when writing children's tales; and in the name Shrii Shrii Ánandamúrti when focusing on spiritual topics. Shrii Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar was the name in which his 5018 Prabháta Saḿgiita songs were published. Those songs, mostly in Bengali, were first published in a series of small booklets, and have now been compiled into ten large Bengali volumes. Translation of those ten volumes into a 25-volume English series is under way, hence Prabháta Saḿgiita (though for the most part not yet available in English) is counted as 25 books in the reckoning of 269.

128 of the 269 books are now available in English. All of them except the two available Prabháta Saḿgiita volumes have been compiled into this Electronic Edition. (Prabháta Saḿgiita, text and audio, will in future be presented separately on another CD.) For more specific remarks about the contents of the various books, see their respective Publisher's Notes within this edition.

The figures of 269 and 128 are for books originally published by Ananda Marga Central Publications. Seven English compilations not originally published by Ananda Marga Central Publications, rather by Ananda Marga Publications at other levels but now the property of Ananda Marga Central Publications and also included in the ElEdit, are Bábá's Grace, The Great Universe, Supreme Expression Volume 1, Supreme Expression Volume 2, Bábá in Fiesch, Bábá in Taiwan and Universal Humanism. Virtually all of their contents duplicates material that has been included among the 128.

ElEdit 7

The ElEdit 7 contains all the 1224 discourses and articles by Shrii P.R. Sarkar that have ever been published in English. ("Discourse" here means that the author originally delivered it as a speech before an audience. "Article" means that the author gave it as dictation to one or a few persons. "Published in English" means that the discourse/article was either originally given in English, or subsequently translated from the original Bengali or Hindi.) The count of 1224 includes as one discourse or article each the long discourses Problems of the Day and To the Patriots; Discourses on Prout, which was really a series of discourses; A Guide to Human Conduct, which is divided into sections; and Sarkar's English Grammar. Many book chapters in the count of discourses/articles/chapters are really excerpts or compilations of excerpts from other discourses; to pare the figure of 1224 to an exact count of original discourses/articles would require a very detailed calculation. Light Comes, a collection of short excerpts published as a book, is counted as one discourse; and The Thoughts of P.R. Sarkar, a similar book, is counted as two discourses ("Spiritual Discourses" and "Social Discourses").

DMC

Normally the most important of the author's discourses on spiritual topics were given on the occasions of DMC. Dharma Mahácakra was a special spiritual gathering addressed by the author as preceptor of Ananda Marga. The inclusion of the September, 1979 Haifa and Kingston DMC's in this ElEdit 7 finally completes the publication in English of all the DMC's that the author gave (all of them in English) outside of India.

Corrections

Only the latest printed versions (as of May 2006) of all the discourses/articles have been reproduced in this Electronic Edition. Moreover, as compared to the printed books, corrections of mistakes in translation or in tape transcription have been made in many discourses/articles. All such changes have been flagged by enclosing them in double square brackets [[   ]]. In addition to such significant changes that have been flagged, obvious spelling, punctuation and typographical mistakes may have been corrected without any such flagging. If apart from corrections of obvious spelling, punctuation and typographical mistakes, any discrepancies between the ElEdit and the printed books are noticed which have not been flagged, the printed books should be taken as authoritative. Please report any such discrepancies to us at support@eledit.net, preferably with a copy to translat@cal3.vsnl.net.in

Source Information

For information about the publication history of any discourse, click (at the top or bottom of the discourse) on the title of the book in which that discourse was published; this will take you to the table of contents of that book. Then see if there is a Publisher's Note for that book. If there is, it may contain source information.

Etymologies of Words

The author used a certain shorthand for explaining the etymologies of words. Under this system, a minus sign (-) follows a prefix, and a plus sign (+) precedes a suffix. Thus ava - tr + ghaiṋ = avatára can be read, "the root tr prefixed by ava and suffixed by ghaiṋ becomes avatára."

Acknowledgements

The English translation and editing of the works of Shrii P.R. Sarkar accomplished to date has been made possible by a great number of persons, almost all of whom volunteered their time and energy, over a period of fifty-one years. It would not be practical to name all such persons here; nor would it even be possible, because full records have not been kept of this history of dedication. Despite the exhausting labour sometimes involved in producing publications materials of good quality, all such persons felt that it was a rare privilege to be allowed to do this work. Perhaps they felt that it was they who owed an acknowledgement to the author. But society should acknowledge its debt to these persons, even if they remain nameless, and heedless of recognition.

Some English Translation Statistics (updated 22 June 06)

As of April '91, we calculated the writings of Ba'ba' (as Shrii P.R. Sarkar is affectionately known), published or scheduled for publication (scheduled, that is, on the basis of known discourses as yet unpublished in book form), to come to 420 titles.* (Of course between some titles there was considerable repetition of material.)

* The 420 included P.R. Sarkar on History, A'darsh Bhojpuri Vya'ka'ran' [Ideal Bhojpuri Grammar] and a book on Angika grammar. P.R. Sarkar on History was originally included among the Ba'ba' books scheduled for publication, and is now, though it eventually came out in Da'da' Raghuna'th's name, included in the 420 as one of Ba'ba''s published books. A'darsh Bhojpuri Vya'ka'ran', though written by Da'da' Shraddha'nanda, was written basically according to instructions given by Ba'ba', hence is also included. A book on Angika grammar similarly commissioned by Ba'ba' has not been written yet, but was inadvertently included when these statistics were first compiled, hence continues to be included for statistical convenience.

That 420 base began to expand in October '94, based partly on rediscovered darshans -- some from tapes, some from old magazines and notes -- and partly on new compilation of existing material. Based on rediscovered darshans, we now have A'nanda Vacana'mrtam Part 24 (Bengali), Parts 25-29 and 32 (Hindi), and Parts 30, 31, 33 and 34 (English); Subha's'ita Sam'graha Parts 22, 23 and 25 (Hindi) and Part 24 (English); and Jiivana Diipika' (Bengali). Based on new compilation, we have (in Bengali) the books Bhaktirasa o Kiirtana-Mahima' [The Flow of Bhakti and the Bliss of Kiirtana], Ba'm'la'r Man'ipradiip Barendrabhu'mi [Barendrabhu'mi: The Jewel-Lamp of Bengal], Prakrta Guru Ke? [The Criteria of a Guru], Dravyagun'e Roga'rogya [Herbal Cures for Disease] and Pra'ut'er Ru'parekha' [An Outline of Prout]; (in Bengali and English) the books Krs'n'atattva o Giita'sa'ra (Discourses on Krs'n'a and the Giita') and Na'riir Marya'da' (The Awakening of Women); and (in English) the books Discourses on Neohumanist Education and Prabha'ta Sam'giita Selected Songs Part 1. 420 + 25 = in all 445 titles. 7 of the original 420 were considered untranslatable (there would be no purpose in translating them) -- e.g., Ba'ba''s Bengali dictionary. So regarding English, the base of books already available in English, or scheduled ultimately for English publication, was 413 and has now grown to 438. The following table shows the translation progress, such as it has been, between April '91 in the left column and June 2006 in the right, for those 438 books (413 of which we calculated for in '91 and 25 of which we didn't):

Calculating the Pace of the English Work

26 new English titles (translations or original English) have come out since April '91: Proutist Economics, Tantra 1 and 2, Discourses on Prout, SS 11, 12, 18, 19, 21 and 24, A'V 23, 30, 31, 33 and 34, P.R. Sarkar on History, The Awakening of Women, Shabda Cayanika' 1-3, Neohumanist Education, Discourses on Krs'n'a and the Giita', Varn'a Vijina'na, Strange Experiences, Ra'r'h: The Cradle of Civilization, Prabha'ta Sam'giita Selected Songs Part 1. (Of course as far as work during that 15-year period, there have also been many reprints/retranslations -- Problems of the Day, To the Patriots, Yoga Psychology (twice revised), I&I, English Grammar, Namah Shiva'ya Sha'nta'ya, CC1, A'nanda Su'tram, Nama'mi Krs'n'asundaram, A'nanda Ma'rga: Elementary Philosophy, Human Society 1 and 2, Liberation of Intellect, Neohumanism in a Nutshell 1, Yogic Treatments -- but these have not affected the statistics.) For purposes of a future projection, it would be best to remember that out of the overall total of 445 titles, 201, as mentioned above, are small Bengali Prabha'ta Sam'giita booklets; and therefore to adjust the figures in terms of the full-size books that will appear in English. The PS booklets will be published in English eight to a volume (a full-size book); they will constitute 25 full-size books. In these terms the overall total comes to 445 - 201 + 25 = 269 books (7 of them untranslatable), and in these terms the above table (which focuses on the English) would appear as follows:

Year 1991 2006
in English (originally in English, or translated into English by that year) 102 128*
still to be translated (including 24 parts of Prabha'ta Sam'giita) 141 131
to be published originally in English 19* 3
Total 262 262

* P.R. Sarkar on History was originally included among the 19 Ba'ba' books due for original publication in English, and is now, though it came out in Da'da' Raghuna'th's name, included among the figure of 128 in English.

141 + 19 = 160 full-size books still to come out in English as of '91. 26 have actually come out in those 15 years, so at the rate of 26 every 15 years, with 134 books (full-size books) still to go, it will take about 77 years to complete the work. This is a strict calculation according to number of untranslated titles. But many of the individual articles in those books have come out in different English compilations; and as mentioned, among some titles there is considerable repetition of material (it would be very detailed to calculate just how much); and we expect the time spent on reprints/retranslations to decrease somewhat. So perhaps 65 years to complete the work, if we maintained the pace of the past fifteen years.

However, under an accelerated-translation project initiated in July of 2005, the translation of six new books is well under way, and we now expect the pace of the past fifteen years to be greatly enhanced.

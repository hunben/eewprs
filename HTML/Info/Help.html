ELECTRONIC EDITION 7.5 – HELP

 

 

Getting Started

Welcome to the Electronic Edition of the Works of P.R. Sarkar, version 7.5.

This edition contains:

  • A set of books and compilations, listed in alphabetical order.
  • A set of individual discourses and articles, listed in alphabetical and chronological order.
  • A list of pre-indexed words for simple, one-word search.
  • A separate application for advanced search.
  • An alternative layout for viewing on small screen devices (mobile phones, PDAs, etc.)

The home page

When you insert the CD, or when you open the Electronic Edition from the start menu, you land on the "home page" of EE7.5.

 

 

You can return to the home page at any time by clicking on the "Home" link at the top left corner of the browser window.

Every page of EE7.5 is accessible from the home page with a few clicks.

The quick navigation links

At the top left of the screen, next to the Home link, there are three more links that quickly take you to the desired position in the navigation bar:

Books takes you to the list of books and discourses.
Words takes you to the list of pre-indexed words.
Help takes you to the list of help pages.

The same items – books, discourses, help pages – can also be accessed by scrolling the navigation bar.

The navigation bar

The vertical navigation bar on the left contains the links to the main areas of the program. You will start most of your searches there:

The navigation bar includes the following sections:

Books contains the list of books.
Discourses contains the alphabetical and chronological list of documents and discourses.
Words contains the list of all words used throughout EE7.5.
Help contains the list of help pages such as this one.

 

The books

The first group of links in the navigation bar takes you to the books area.

Finding a book

The books are listed by title in alphabetical order.

You can click on the word "Books" in the navigation bar and scroll down until you find the book you are looking for.

You can also click on the first letter of the title and reach that position in the list of titles.

Opening a book

In the list, each title is followed by the list of its chapters. You can click on the title to open the book at the beginning, or you can click on a particular chapter to go straight there.

Inside a book

Each book is contained in a single, long HTML page.

At the top of the book page is the title and a list of the chapters. You can click on a chapter to go straight there, or use the scroll bar to scroll down.

Discourses

Below the books links in the navigation bar are the links to the individual discourses. The discourses are listed twice, in alphabetical and in chronological order.

Click on the "Alphabetical" link to open the list at the beginning, or click on a letter to jump to the titles starting with it.

Similarly, click on "Chronological" to open the list at the beginning, or on a number (representing the year, i.e. 55 = 1955, etc.)

Discourses with no available date are listed under "Undated".

From the discourse lists you can go to each individual discourse (click on the title) or go to the books in which the discourse is included (click on the book titles)

The EE7.5 Search application

EE7.5 includes a Windows program for advanced text search. You can use it to search the entire Electronic Edition for exact phrases, partial words, etc.

You can use the search program from the CD itself or install it on your computer with the text.

In the CD, the search program is in the X:\Windows\Search folder, where X is the letter of your CD drive.

If your computer has the CD Autorun feature enabled, a EE7.5 welcome menu appears automatically when you insert the CD, and you can click the "Search Text on CD" button to run the search program.

If you install the EE7.5 Search program on your computer you can then run it from the Start menu: Start\Programs\Electronic Edition 7\Search

See the program's own help page for detailed instructions on how to conduct searches.

Autorun application

If your computer has the CD Autorun feature enabled, a EE7.5 welcome menu appears automatically when you insert the CD in the drive.

From there you can open the EE7.5 home page, launch the search program, or start installing the EE7.5 files and programs on your computer.

 

Install EE7.5 on your computer

Windows:

  • Open the CD with Windows Explorer and open the folder X:\Windows\Setup (where X is the letter of your CD drive).

  • Run the Install program (file Install.exe)

  • The installer will give you the option to choose exactly what you want to install. You can run the installation again later to add components.

  • After the installer program finishes copying the files you will be able to access the Electronic Edition from Windows' the Start menu (Start\Programs\Electronic Edition 7)

    Mac, Linux, and other Operating Systems

  • Insert the CD and browse into the folder named HTML. You will find a single file there, named index.html, and several subfolders (Books, Discourses, Images, etc.)

  • Create an empty folder on your computer into which you'll install EE7.5.

  • (A) If you want a complete installation, which will take about 500 MB of disk space, copy the entire content of the CD's HTML folder into your new folder.

  • (B) If you want to use less than 100 MB of disk space on your computer, copy the entire content of the HTML folder, except the sub-folder named Words. You will still have all books and discourses and navigation files, but you will not have the pre-indexed words' files, which take over 400 MB of disk space.

  • Open the Electronic Edition by opening the index.html file. You may create a shortcut to this file to open EE7.5 without having to browse to this folder every time.

  • For advanced text search, use any text indexing and search application available for your operating system.

     

    Ucis and Nicis

    P.R. Sarkar's "Roman Sanskrit" system of writing Sanskrit with Roman letters, two types of diacritical marks are used: the Uci and the Nici.

    Uci stands for "Urdhva cihna" which means "mark above". It's a small sign, similar to an acute accent, placed above letters a, u, r, t, d, n and s, turning them into á ú ŕ t́ d́ ń ḿ ś.

    In this edition the Unicode character U+0301 has been used as the uci mark. When writing in plain ASCII (e.g. in an email) an apostrophe can be used in lieu of the uci: a', u', r', t', d', n', m', s'. This is also how Roman Sanskrit used to be written in prior versions of the Electronic Edition.

    Nici stands for "Nimna cihna", which means "mark below". The nici is only used below the letter N, and it has the shape of a circumflex accent: Ṋ, ṋ.

    When writing in plain ASCII the nici is usually omitted, and it can usually be inferred from the context. For example, the word "Jiṋána" would be written "Jina'na". When one must indicate a of a nici, the ASCII character "^" may be used after the n, in which case the word "Jiṋána" would be written "Jin^a'na".

    Using only these two simple signs, uci and nici, it is possible to romanize correctly and without ambiguity not only the Sanskrit vocabulary, but also other Indic scripts such as Bengali, Devanagari, Urdu, etc.

     

    Fonts

    Do the following characters...

    á ú ŕ t́ d́ ń ṋ ḿ ś
    Á Ú Ŕ T́ D́ Ń Ṋ Ḿ Ś

    ...look like this?

    And do the following characters...

    আনন্দ মার্গ

    ...look like this?

    If the characters above look like those on the yellow background (small differences in the position of the marks or in the shape of the letters are OK) then you already have all the necessary fonts installed, and you don't need to do anything.

    If however some of the above characters are not displayed properly, you need to install (or upgrade) the proper fonts.

    The fonts needed are two: Tahoma, for Roman letters, and Rupali, for Indic (Bengali and Devanagari) letters.

    These fonts are included on the CD and here is how to install them:

    Windows

    Open the folder named "Fonts" in the main Windows folder. Usually it will be C:\WINDOWS\FONTS

    If you have a font named Tahoma, delete it first. (You need to first delete the old version before you install a new one).

    (You may have to do the same for other fonts if an older version is present on your system and Windows gives you a warning.)

    Then click on File | Install new font...

    Then browse to the CD drive, open the folder Fonts, select all the fonts listed there, make sure the option Copy fonts to Fonts folder is selected, and click on OK.

    Once the fonts are installed you may refresh this page (F5) and you should be able to see all characters properly. (In some cases the proper characters may only show up after you restart Windows)

    MacIntosh

    Insert the CD.

    Open the Fonts folder on the CD

    Open the Fonts folder on the Mac.

    Search for the Tahoma font on your Mac, and if you find it, delete it.

    Copy the font files from the CD to the Mac, and they will be installed.

    Note: If you do not delete an older version of Tahoma or other fonts, OSX will not replace it with the new one, but will add the new one with the name Tahoma2. After that there will be two fonts with the same name, and the system may or may not choose the right one to display the characters. Therefore it's necessary to delete the old font before installing the new one.

     

    Copy and Paste

    You can do common clipboard copy-and-paste operations from these pages to word processors, spreadsheets, etc.

    If however in the selected text there are characters with ucis (Á, Ú etc.) and nicis (ṋ and Ṋ), then, after you paste the text into a word processor, you will have to select a proper Unicode font (Tahoma, Arial Unicode, etc.) capable of rendering these diacritical marks.

     

    EE7.5 on mobile phones and other devices with small screens

    Mobile phones, smartphones, PDAs, and other devices equipped with a web browser can be used to read this edition of P.R. Sarkar's books and discourses.

    Because these devices have small screens, a simplified page layout is available, so that the full width of the screen is available for reading while a thin, simplified navigation bar remais visible at the top of the screen.

    You can access the simplified layout from the home page, clicking the link Small screen view

    You can return to the full layout by clicking on Full screen view in the simplified layout's home page.

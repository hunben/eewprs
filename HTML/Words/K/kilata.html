Kit́i to Kiisha (Discourse 26) (6)

[35] Kilát́a

[36] Kila + at́ac = kilát́a . Etymologically kilát́a means “that which has been whitened”. In common usage kilát́a means the following: Freshly washed, immaculately white cloth.

[38] Panir (cheese). Cheese is also called kilát́a . The word panir is of Persian root – “cheese” is English.

[39] If you wish, you can easily use the word kilát́a for panir or cheese, in Bengali or in other Indian languages.
